**The honest answer: it depends entirely on which bootcamp and which candidate.** A well-run IT bootcamp with strong employer partnerships can compress 12–18 months of self-directed learning into 3–6 months with built-in accountability and job placement support. A poorly run bootcamp is an expensive way to watch someone else study. The difference between them is not obvious from marketing materials.

*Cost should not be your primary concern when evaluating a bootcamp — outcomes should be. A $10,000 bootcamp that places 80% of graduates is a better investment than a $3,000 bootcamp that places 20%.*

## What Good IT Bootcamps Provide

- **Structure and accountability** — a schedule, instructors, and a cohort of peers who motivate consistent progress
- **Employer partnerships** — direct relationships with hiring managers who actively recruit from the program's graduates
- **Job placement support** — resume review, interview prep, career coaching, and introductions
- **Compressed timelines** — covering certification prep material plus hands-on labs in a structured full-time format
- **Current curriculum** — the best bootcamps continuously update material to match current hiring demand

## What Bootcamps Do Not Provide

- **Vendor certification exams** — these are taken separately; a bootcamp prepares you but the exam cost is separate
- **Deep technical expertise** — bootcamps build breadth and interview readiness, not the deep expertise that comes from years of practice
- **Any guarantee of employment** — reputable bootcamps publish employment outcome statistics; unreputable ones do not

## When a Bootcamp Is Worth It

A bootcamp makes most sense when:

- You learn best with structure and accountability rather than self-direction
- You have limited time and want to compress the path to employment
- The bootcamp has verifiable employment outcome data (60%+ placement rate within 6 months in IT-adjacent roles)
- The total cost is affordable relative to the salary increase you are targeting
- Your target role aligns with what the bootcamp employers actually hire

## When Self-Study + Certifications Beats a Bootcamp

Skip the bootcamp and pursue certifications independently when:

- You are self-disciplined and learn well from video courses and labs
- You have 12–18 months to steadily self-study
- The bootcamp cost is significant relative to your budget
- The bootcamp's employer partnerships do not align with your target roles or geography

The CompTIA A+ + Network+ + Security+ stack combined with a documented home lab provides equivalent or better credentials than many bootcamp completions at a fraction of the cost.

## Questions to Ask Any Bootcamp Before Enrolling

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| What is your 6-month job placement rate, and in what roles? | The real measure of outcomes |
| What employers have hired your graduates in the last 6 months? | Verifies employer partnerships are real |
| What certifications are students prepared for, and what is the pass rate? | Credential value |
| Is tuition refunded or income-share-agreement based if I do not get placed? | Alignment of incentives |
| Can I speak with three recent graduates? | The most reliable source of truth |

## Notable IT Bootcamp Types

| Type | Examples | Typical Focus |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **CompTIA prep bootcamps** | Various local/online programs | A+, Network+, Security+ in 8–12 weeks |
| **Cybersecurity bootcamps** | SANS Cyber Academy, SecureSet (defunct), CISA Partner programs | Security analyst, SOC analyst roles |
| **Cloud bootcamps** | Cloud Institute, various AWS/Azure prep programs | Cloud practitioner through associate certs |
| **General tech programs** | Google IT Support Certificate (Coursera) | Help desk and IT support entry-level |
