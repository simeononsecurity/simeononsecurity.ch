**Each section of your resume has specific rules about content, order, and depth.** Knowing what to include — and what to leave out — in each section is as important as writing quality bullets.

*This module takes about 12 minutes. After reading it, you'll know exactly what to include in your Experience, Projects, and Education sections and how to handle the hard cases.*

______

## Experience Section

### Order: Most Recent First, Always

Reverse chronological order is standard and expected. ATS systems are built around it. Recruiters expect it. Functional resumes (skills-first, no dates) score poorly in ATS and look like candidates are hiding something.

*If you have a gap or a non-linear career path, handle it directly — don't rearrange the section order to obscure it.*

### What to Include Per Role

For each position, include:

- **Company name** (bold)
- **Your title** (bold)
- **Location** — city/state or "Remote"
- **Dates** — month and year, start and end, consistent format
- **3–5 bullet points** focused on accomplishments (from Module 4)
- **Tech stack or tools** — can be in a brief line below the title or woven into bullets

```
Acme Corp — Senior Software Engineer — Remote
Jan 2022 – Present

• Built real-time event pipeline processing 12M daily events, reducing processing latency from 8s to 400ms
• Led migration from monolith to microservices for 3 core services, enabling independent deployment cadences
• Mentored 4 junior engineers through career development plans; 2 promoted within 18 months
• Reduced on-call alert volume 62% by implementing structured runbooks and improving monitoring coverage
Stack: Go, Kafka, Kubernetes, AWS (ECS, RDS, CloudWatch), Terraform
```

### How Many Roles to Include

Go back 10–15 years for experienced candidates. Roles older than 15 years rarely add value unless they're uniquely relevant. A 2-line abbreviated entry is fine for old roles:

```
Early Career: QA Engineer at various startups (2004–2009)
```

### Gaps in Employment

Do not leave unexplained gaps of 6+ months. A brief note is always better than silence.

**Acceptable gap notes:**

- `Career break — family caregiving, 2023–2024`
- `Independent consulting / freelance, 2022–2023`
- `Full-time bootcamp / credential program, 2021`
- `Sabbatical — travel and open-source contributions, 2020`

**The year-only trap**: Some candidates use only years (2019–2021) to hide a short gap between roles. Recruiters know this trick and will ask. Use month-year throughout and address gaps head-on.

### Contract, Freelance, and Consulting Work

List it. Contract work counts as professional experience.

If you have many short contracts, group them:

```
Independent Consultant — Software Engineering
Jan 2021 – Dec 2022

Clients included: Stripe (infrastructure audit), Notion (API scaling), two seed-stage startups
• Led infrastructure design for Stripe's internal tooling migration affecting 200+ engineers
• Defined API rate limiting strategy for Notion, reducing abuse incidents 40%
```

If each contract has enough material for its own entry, list them individually with the client name as the company and the body for accomplishments.

## Projects Section

### When Projects Matter Most

The projects section is the most underused part of a resume — especially for early-career candidates, career changers, and technical candidates.

Use it when:
- You're applying to technical roles and want to demonstrate hands-on skills
- You're early-career with limited professional experience
- You're switching industries and want to show applied skills in the new domain
- The role requires a specific technology you haven't used professionally but have built something with

For senior candidates with 10+ years, the projects section is usually optional unless the projects directly support the application (major open-source contributions, published tools with real usage).

### Where to Put It

- **New grad / entry-level**: After Skills, before or after Education (above Experience if education is most relevant)
- **Mid-level with relevant projects**: After Experience
- **Senior**: Omit unless highly relevant

### What to Include Per Project

For each project include:
- **Project name** — linked to GitHub or live URL if available
- **1–2 sentences**: what it does and why it exists
- **Technologies used**
- **Scale or outcome** if measurable (users, stars, deployment status)

**Example:**

> **OpenBudget** — [github.com/yourname/openbudget](https://github.com)
> Personal finance tracker built with React and a Go backend, deployed on Fly.io. ~200 active users since launch.
> *Stack: React, Go, PostgreSQL, Plaid API, Fly.io*

### What to Skip

**Tutorial projects** — "Todo App," "Weather App," "Netflix Clone" — these signal learning, not genuine skill application. Replace them with something you built to solve a real problem.

**Unfinished projects with empty repos.** If the GitHub link goes to a repo with 2 commits and no README, leave it off.

**Outdated tech stack.** If the project is 7 years old and uses frameworks you no longer know, it won't help in a technical screen.

## Education Section

### Where to Put It

The placement depends entirely on your career stage:

| Stage | Placement |
|-------|----------|
| **Recent grad (0–3 years)** | Above experience, right below summary |
| **Early career (3–5 years)** | Between experience and skills, or after |
| **Experienced (5+ years)** | Below experience — your work history is more important |
| **Academic / PhD** | Above experience with full publication/research section |

### What to Include

- **Degree** — full name: "Bachelor of Science in Computer Science" (not "B.S. CS")
- **Major** — and minor if relevant
- **University name** — full name, not abbreviation
- **Graduation year** — just the year is fine
- **GPA** — include only if 3.5 or above and within the last 5 years
- **Relevant coursework** — only if directly relevant and you lack professional experience in that area
- **Honors / Dean's List / leadership** — include if substantive, recent, and not overloaded

**Example:**

```
University of Michigan — B.S. Computer Science, May 2022
GPA: 3.7 | Dean's List (6 semesters) | Teaching Assistant, Algorithms (2021–2022)
```

### What to Skip

Once you have a college degree: drop high school entirely.

Once you're past 5 years of experience: drop GPA unless it was exceptionally high and still relevant.

Don't list the university's street address.

Don't include coursework that's just the standard curriculum for your degree — "took data structures" isn't a differentiator.

## Section Order by Career Stage

| Career Stage | Recommended Order |
|-------------|------------------|
| **New grad / Student** | Contact → Summary → Education → Projects → Skills → Experience |
| **Entry-level (0–2 yrs)** | Contact → Summary → Skills → Education → Projects → Experience |
| **Mid-level (2–5 yrs)** | Contact → Summary → Experience → Skills → Projects → Education |
| **Senior (5+ yrs)** | Contact → Summary → Experience → Skills → Education |
| **Career changer** | Contact → Summary → Transferable Skills → Education → Experience |
| **Academic / PhD** | Contact → Summary → Education → Research → Publications → Experience |
