**A generic resume will always underperform a tailored one.** ATS systems compare your resume to the specific job posting at the time of submission. A resume tuned for a different role — even one in the same field — scores lower because the vocabulary doesn't match.

*This module takes about 10 minutes. After reading it, you'll have a specific, repeatable workflow for tailoring any resume to any job description.*

______

## The Hard Truth About Generic Resumes

Most job seekers maintain one resume and submit it to every job. This is understandable — tailoring takes time. But ATS systems measure keyword coverage against the exact text of each posting.

Two candidates apply for the same role:
- **Candidate A**: Generic resume, 45% keyword match
- **Candidate B**: Tailored resume, 73% keyword match

Candidate B appears higher in the recruiter's queue. They may have equivalent experience. The difference is vocabulary alignment.

*The goal is not to fake experience. It's to describe genuine experience using the specific vocabulary the job description uses.*

______

## What Tailoring Actually Means

Tailoring a resume does not mean rewriting everything from scratch for every application. That's unsustainable and unnecessary.

It means making five targeted changes:

### 1. Swap or Add Keywords in Your Summary

Your summary appears at the top and carries extra weight in many ATS systems. If the job uses "platform engineering" and your summary says "infrastructure engineering," swap it.

This takes 2–3 minutes and has significant impact on ATS scoring.

### 2. Reorder Bullets Within Each Role

Put the most relevant bullets first. A recruiter reading your work history scans the first 1–2 bullets per role. If your most relevant accomplishment is buried at #4, it may not get read.

**No rewriting required** — just reorder.

### 3. Adjust Your Summary to Reflect the Specific Role

Your summary should name the domain and specialty this specific company is hiring for — not a generic description of your career. A 30-second edit to the summary sentence targeting the company's language makes the section read as relevant instead of general.

### 4. Add or Remove the Projects Section

If the role strongly values hands-on technical work and your projects are relevant, add the projects section. If the role is focused on seniority and leadership and the projects are dated, remove them.

This decision requires one judgment call, not a rewrite.

### 5. Check Your Job Title Parenthetical

If your job title doesn't match industry standard language, add a parenthetical. "Platform Engineer (DevOps/SRE equivalent)" matches more keyword patterns than "Platform Engineer" alone.

## What Tailoring Does NOT Mean

- **Making up experience you don't have** — your resume leads to an interview; the interview tests what you claimed
- **Completely rewriting bullets for every job** — if your core experience is strong, a vocabulary adjustment is enough
- **Using a different resume for every single application** — maintain 3–4 base versions by role type, then do light tailoring per application

______

## The Three-Version System

Maintain 3–4 base resume versions for different role types, then do light tailoring per application.

**Example versions:**

| Base Version | When to Use |
|-------------|------------|
| **IC/Technical** | Individual contributor engineering, data, product roles |
| **Management/Leadership** | Director, VP, team lead, people management roles |
| **Early-career/Projects** | Roles where education and projects outweigh experience |
| **Career-change** | Emphasizes transferable skills and reframes experience for new domain |

Light tailoring on any of these takes 10–15 minutes per application. Full rewriting is unnecessary when the base version is well-constructed.

## The 70% Threshold

Run the [ATS Resume Improver](https://atsresumeimprover.netlify.app/) keyword gap check on your tailored resume before submitting.

The practical benchmark:
- **Below 60%**: Worth another keyword pass before submitting. Check the gap list for critical missing terms.
- **60–70%**: Acceptable for most applications. Quick scan for easy misses.
- **70%+**: Well-aligned. Human readability is now the deciding factor.
- **Above 85%**: Verify you haven't keyword-stuffed — read it out loud and confirm it flows naturally.

*The score is a diagnostic tool. Use it to identify gaps, not as a pass/fail gate.*

______

## A Repeatable Tailoring Workflow

For each application:

1. **Save the job description** to a text file (postings get taken down after you apply)
2. **Open the ATS Resume Improver** and run a baseline keyword check on your current base version
3. **Note the top 5 critical gaps** — terms that appear in the job description but not in your resume
4. **Add missing terms** to your summary, skills section, or bullets where accurate
5. **Reorder 1–2 bullets per role** to put the most relevant accomplishment first
6. **Adjust your summary** to name the exact domain/specialty this role requires
7. **Run the check again** — verify the score improved and the text still reads naturally
8. **Save a renamed copy** as `FirstLast_Resume_[Company]_[Date].pdf`

This workflow takes 15–20 minutes per application. Doing it consistently and thoroughly is more effective than submitting more applications with a generic resume.

## Vocabulary Substitution Examples

Many candidates have the right experience but use the wrong words:

| Your Term | Job Description Term | Change Needed |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| "containerized deployments" | "Docker" | Add "Docker" |
| "cloud computing" | "AWS" | Specify the platform |
| "data pipelines" | "ETL" | Add "ETL" |
| "continuous delivery" | "CI/CD" | Use both: "CI/CD (continuous delivery)" |
| "web development" | "React, Node.js" | Name the specific stack |
| "statistical analysis" | "Python, pandas" | Name the tools |

Each of these substitutions takes 30 seconds. Each one directly improves your keyword match score.
